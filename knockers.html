RE: Knocking Sounds – Theory, Not Final

This one’s older than the mine itself. You hear tapping once and you shrug it off. You hear it twice and it starts following a pattern. A third time, and it stops when you speak. That’s when you know you’re not alone down there.

Locals called them “Knockers.” Appalachian miners said “Tommyknockers,” likely borrowed from Cornish tales carried over by old world hands. It’s always something — fae, echoes, trapped air, the earth itself shifting. We give names to what we can't map. Doesn’t mean it's not real.

I've found notes about offerings. Cornwall, early shafts — they left scraps of their lunch. Mincemeat, crust, a bite of pasty. Superstition, maybe. Or maybe they were paying toll. Not to stay safe—but to be allowed to leave.

I’ll be back near the site in a week. Planning to get eyes on Shaft 4, maybe even further if it hasn’t collapsed. Just want to see for myself. These things loop around in your head if you don’t.

I’ll leave something behind — nothing big. Just a crust and a whisper. If I don’t post again, maybe it’s because nothing happened. Or maybe it’s because something did.
